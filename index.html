About Me

I am a global health research professional with a Master's degree in Global Public Health from New York University (GPA: 3.96/4.00) and a Bachelor's in Molecular and Cellular Biology from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (GPA: 3.74/4.00).

Currently working as a Research Associate at Weill Cornell Medicine's Center for Global Health, I develop grant proposals, manage data using REDCap, and conduct qualitative research across diverse global health initiatives, including HIV prevention in rural Uganda and GDM screening policy changes in New York and India.

I am certified as a CAPM (Certified Associate in Project Management) and have contributed to multiple peer-reviewed publications and conference presentations in the fields of global health, HIV prevention, gestational diabetes, and dementia care.
